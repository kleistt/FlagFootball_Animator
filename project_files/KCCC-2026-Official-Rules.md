## Tournament Structure

- Each company may enter **one** coed team; teams compete only against companies in their division.
- A team representative must **check in no less than 15 minutes prior** to scheduled game time and confirm enough participants to play.
- Team rosters are not required, but only players registered for KCCC who have signed the KCCC waiver are eligible. Random eligibility checks occur; **photo ID must be available at game time**. Failure to show ID on check results in a **forfeit plus associated point penalties**.
- Teams may have an unlimited number of participants; all must be legal players from the company's participation list.
- **10 flag belts are provided** and must be used. A game ball is provided (intermediate/junior size) but teams may use their own.
- Format: **5v5**, with **at least 2 women on the field at all times**. Teams may start with 4 players but must still have 2 women playing.
- **Scheduled game time is forfeit time.** Be at the field before scheduled time.
- **Single elimination** tournament.
- **Two officials** per game.
- Governing rules: **Official USSSA Flag Football Certified Rules for 2026**, modified by the 5v5 exceptions in this document.
- Protests and illegal-player disputes: see the **KCCC Protest Policy**.

## The Game

- **No contact allowed. NO BLOCKING.**
- A coin toss determines first possession.
- **Ball must be on the ground at the snap.** Any incomplete snap is a dead ball.
- **Snaps between the legs or to the side are allowed.** (Shotgun is *not* required — side snaps are also legal.)
- Offense starts each drive at its own **5-yard line** and has **three (3) plays to cross midfield**. After crossing midfield, the offense has **three (3) plays to score a touchdown**.
- If the offense fails to cross midfield (or to score after crossing), **possession changes** and the opposing team starts its drive at its own 5-yard line.
- All possession changes **except interceptions** start at the new offense's 5-yard line.
- Games are **two 12-minute halves with a two-minute halftime**. Teams change sides after the first half, and possession changes.
- **25-second play clock** after the ball is spotted. One warning is given before a delay-of-game penalty is enforced.

---

## Scoring

- Games end at **28 points** or at **24 minutes** (end of second half), whichever comes first.
- **Touchdown:** 6 points.
- **Extra point:** 1 point from 5 yards out, **or** 2 points from 12 yards out.
- **Safety:** 2 points.

---

## Time-Outs

- Each team has **one 60-second time-out per game**. The clock stops during the time-out.

---

## Attire

- No metal cleats. Footwear for artificial turf is suggested.
- **Shirts must be tucked in.**
- **No pockets, no belt loops** on pants or shorts.
- Protective mouthpieces are suggested for all players.
- No jewelry. Tape may cover jewelry that cannot be removed.

---

## Rushing the Quarterback

- Every rusher must be **a minimum of 7 yards from the line of scrimmage at the snap**. Any number of players may rush.
- Players *not* rushing may defend the line of scrimmage.
- **Jumping to block a pass is allowed**, but any contact with the QB during the jump or on landing is a penalty.
- Once the ball has been **handed off**, the 7-yard rule no longer applies — **all defenders become eligible to rush**.
- A marker (or the referee) will designate the 7-yard line.

---

## Running

- **The quarterback cannot run the ball — including a female QB.**
- Only **direct hand-offs behind the line of scrimmage** are legal. **No laterals or pitches of any kind.** Multiple hand-offs are allowed.
- The player who takes the hand-off **may throw** the ball as long as they do not cross the line of scrimmage before throwing.
- **No-Run Zones** are located:
  - 5 yards before midfield (in each direction)
  - 5 yards before each end zone
- No-Run Zones exist to **avoid short-yardage power situations**.
- Ball is spotted where the ball-carrier's **belt** is when the flag is pulled — **not** where the ball is. Ball and flags must **break the plane** for a first down and touchdown.
- **A male player may not advance the ball across the line of scrimmage.**
- **Spinning is allowed**, but players **cannot leave their feet** to avoid a defender (no diving with the ball).

---

## Passing

- All passes must be **forward** and received **beyond the line of scrimmage**.
- **Shovel passes are allowed**, but must be received beyond the line of scrimmage.
- The QB has a **7-second pass clock**. If no pass is thrown within 7 seconds, the play is dead, loss of down, ball returns to the LOS.
- Once the ball is **handed off**, the 7-second rule no longer applies.

---

## Receiving

- **All players are eligible receivers** — including the QB, *if* the ball has been handed off behind the line of scrimmage first.
- **Only one player may be in motion** at a time before the snap, and that player **may not be moving toward the line of scrimmage at the time of the snap**.
- Receivers must have **at least one foot in bounds** to make a catch.

---

## Dead Balls

Play is dead when any of the following occurs:

- Offensive player's flag is pulled.
- Ball-carrier steps out of bounds.
- Touchdown is scored.
- **Point of interception** — *interception returns are not allowed.*
- Ball-carrier's knee, hand, or the ball hits the ground.
- Ball-carrier's flag falls out.
- Receiver's flag falls out (play is dead at the point of reception).

Additionally:

- **There are no fumbles.** Any time the ball touches the ground, it is dead and spotted where it hit.

---

## Sportsmanship

- No tackling, elbowing, cheap shots, blocking, or any unsportsmanlike conduct.
- If a monitor or referee witnesses any of the above, **the game is stopped and the player is ejected**. Tournament directors may eject a player from the entire tournament.
- **Foul play will not be tolerated.**

---

## Coed Considerations — Open/Closed Plays

Every play is designated as either **Open** or **Closed**.

### Open Play

- The offense may pass the ball from **any player to any player** (male or female) without penalty.
- A **female** is still the only player allowed to advance the ball across the line of scrimmage by rushing.

### Closed Play

- A **female must be involved in the transaction of the ball beyond the line of scrimmage** — as the passer, the receiver, or the non-quarterback rusher.
- **Examples of closed plays:**
  - Completed pass from a male QB to a female receiver (must be beyond LOS).
  - Completed pass from a female QB to a male receiver (must be beyond LOS).
  - QB hand-off to a female running back who advances beyond LOS.

### Determining the Next Play's Status

- If the previous play **involved a female in a completed successful play** (passer, receiver, or rusher), the **next play is Open**.
- If the previous play had a **male passer AND male receiver in a completed successful play**, the **next play is Closed**.
- If an Open play was **unsuccessful** (did not advance the ball beyond LOS), the **next play is still Open**.
- If a Closed play was **unsuccessful** (did not advance the ball beyond LOS), the **next play is still Closed**.
- The referee designates the status of each play **once the ball is spotted and marked ready for play**.

## Overtime Procedures

### Entering Overtime

- If the score is tied at the end of 24 minutes, teams move directly into overtime.
- Coin toss determines order of possession.

### Regular Overtime (all games except Championship and "If Necessary")

- Each team receives **one (1) play** from the 5-yard line, going toward the opposite end zone.
- The team gaining the most yards on its one play wins and is awarded **one additional point**.
- **Negative yardage** is better than an interception, but *not* better than an incomplete pass.
- Defensive penalties may be accepted: result is **10 yards and the option to run an additional play**. The result of the following play and the 10-yard penalty both count toward total possession yardage.
- Offensive penalty: **–10 yards and loss of down**.

### Championship and "If Necessary" Games Overtime

- Each team gets one possession per overtime period. Possession ends on:
  - Loss of possession on downs
  - Interception
  - Successful touchdown
- Each team starts at **midline, 1st and Goal**.
- Each team has **3 plays to score** a touchdown. If they score, they attempt a **2-point conversion**.
- If still tied at the end of an overtime period, play another.
- Order of possession **alternates** each OT period (team 1st in OT 1 is 2nd in OT 2, etc.).
- **All extra-point conversions in this format are 2-point attempts from the 12-yard mark.**

## Penalties

- All penalties are called by the referee and **may be declined**.
- All penalties are assessed from the **original line of scrimmage**.
- Within 10 yards of the goal, the ball is placed at **½ the distance to the goal**.
- **Games cannot end on a defensive penalty** unless the offense declines it.
- Only the **team captain** may ask the referee questions.
- Referees determine incidental contact, which may result from normal play.

### Defensive Penalties

| Penalty | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| Offsides | — |
| Interference | — |
| Illegal Contact | Includes holding, bump-and-run, blocking, jumping to defend pass |
| Illegal Flag Pull | Before receiver has the ball |
| Illegal Rushing | Starting rush from inside the 7-yard marker |
| Unsportsmanlike Conduct | — |

- **All defensive penalties: 10 yards (from LOS) and automatic first down.**
- **All defensive penalties result in the next play being Open.**

### Offensive Penalties

| Penalty | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| Illegal Motion | More than one person moving, false start, motion violations |
| Illegal Forward Pass | Pass received behind the line of scrimmage |
| Flag Guarding | — |
| Jumping by ball carrier | Any player with possession cannot jump; ball is spotted at the spot of the jump |
| Delay of Game | Clock stops; 10 yards and loss of down |
| Offensive Pass Interference | Illegal pick play, receiver pushing defender |
| Unsportsmanlike Conduct | — |

- **All offensive penalties: 10 yards (from LOS) and loss of down.**
- **All offensive penalties preserve the Open/Closed status of the preceding (penalized) play.**

## The Field

- **Field dimensions:** **54 yards long × 22 yards wide, including both end zones.**
- **End zones:** 7 yards deep on each end (so the play area between goal lines is **40 yards**).
- **Midfield** sits 20 yd from each goal line — 27 yd from the back of each end zone.
- **No-Run Zones** (NRZ): four 5-yard bands where a direct run across the LOS is not permitted:
  - Own goal line → +5 yd (NRZ 1)
  - 5 yd before midfield (NRZ 2)
  - Midfield → +5 yd (NRZ 3)
  - 5 yd before AWAY goal line (NRZ 4)

```
        7     5      10       5    5      10       5    7
      <-->  <-->  <------>  <-->  <-->  <------>  <-->  <-->
   +------+-----+----------+-----+-----+----------+-----+------+
   |  END | NRZ |          | NRZ | NRZ |          | NRZ |  END |
   | ZONE |  1  |   open   |  2  |  3  |   open   |  4  | ZONE |
   +------+-----+----------+-----+-----+----------+-----+------+
                                    ^
                                 MIDFIELD (27 yd from back of either EZ)
   |<------------------- 54 yards total (incl. end zones) -------------->|
          |<----------- 40 yards play area (goal-to-goal) ----------->|
   Field width: 22 yards
```

The NRZ segments (5 + 10 + 5 + 5 + 10 + 5 = 40 yd) plus the two 7 yd end zones account for the full 54 yd length.
