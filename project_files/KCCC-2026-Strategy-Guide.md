## 2. The Open/Closed Toggle System

This is the single most important strategic concept in KCCC coed flag football. Master it and you control the game.

### What Is It?

Every play is designated either **OPEN** or **CLOSED** by the referee before the snap. The designation is determined by what happened on the *previous* play.

### Open Play Rules

- Any player may pass to any player (male-to-male is legal)
- A female is still the only player who can rush across the LOS
- Males still cannot advance the ball across the LOS by running

### Closed Play Rules

- A **female must be involved** in the transaction of the ball beyond the LOS
- The female must be one of these three: the passer, the receiver, or the non-QB rusher
- Examples of legal Closed plays:
  - Male QB throws to female receiver (she catches beyond LOS)
  - Female QB throws to male receiver (he catches beyond LOS)
  - QB hands off to female, she runs past LOS

### The Toggle Decision Tree

```
PREVIOUS PLAY                                    NEXT PLAY STATUS
--------------------------------------------------------------
Completed + female involved (any role)        --> OPEN
Completed + male passer to male receiver      --> CLOSED
  (no female involved)
Incomplete / unsuccessful play                --> STAYS THE SAME
Defensive penalty accepted                    --> OPEN
Offensive penalty                             --> STAYS THE SAME
Start of game / start of possession           --> CLOSED (default)
```

### Play-by-Play Toggle Map

| Status Required | Plays Available | Next Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Closed-eligible** | Storm, Lightning, Screen Queen, Jet Sweep Fake, Orbit, Boomerang, Out, Levels, Scissors, Trips, Motion Hand-off, Motion Slants | OPEN |
| **Open-only** | Thunder, Flood Right, Stack Release, Rollout Choice, Fades, Wheel | OPEN (stays Open) |
| **Open-only (flips Closed)** | Mismatch Motion, Motion Keep | CLOSED |

### The "Perpetual Open" Strategy

**Goal: Never let the toggle flip to Closed.**

On every Open play, ensure a female is involved in the completion:
- Throw TO a female receiver, OR
- Have a female throw (double pass / hand-off then throw), OR
- Hand off to a female rusher

If you complete to a female on every Open play, you stay Open *forever*. This eliminates all Closed-play restrictions and gives your offense maximum flexibility.

### When to Deliberately Accept Closed

- When the defense starts cheating on your male receivers because they expect female involvement
- To set up a mismatch: Closed forces the defense to account for the female, which can open up the following Open play
- When your female players are the strongest playmakers anyway and Closed plays don't limit you
- After a defensive penalty (next play is Open regardless), you can plan a Closed completion to set up a specific sequence
- **Mismatch Motion** and **Motion Keep** are Open-only plays that intentionally complete male-to-male, flipping to Closed. Use them when the Closed completion that follows is part of a planned two-play sequence.

## 3. Role Assignments (3 Males + 2 Females)

| Role | Gender | Primary Job | Secondary Job |
|------|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **QB** | Male | Pass, read defense, manage play clock | Hand off, direct traffic, call Open/Closed |
| **M1** | Male | Primary deep/intermediate receiver | Clear out coverage, draw safety attention |
| **M2** | Male | Crossing routes, possession catches | Curl/comeback specialist, safety valve |
| **F1** | Female | **TRIPLE THREAT**: rusher, receiver, double-pass thrower | Most important player on the field |
| **F2** | Female | Motion decoy, outlet receiver, fake rush | Screen target, short zone settler |

### F1: The Engine of the Offense

F1 must be your most athletic and football-smart female player. She will:
1. **Rush the QB** on defense (only females can cross the LOS to rush)
2. **Catch passes** in Closed plays to keep the toggle flipping to Open
3. **Throw the double pass** -- take a hand-off from QB, then throw downfield while still behind LOS
4. **Run the ball** on hand-off plays in the No-Run Zone (where passing is still legal but running creates confusion)

### F2: The Pressure Multiplier

F2 creates doubt in the defense:
- Lines up in motion to pull a defender out of position
- Fakes the rush to freeze the QB spy
- Sits in short zones as a reliable outlet on Closed plays
- Acts as a decoy rusher while F1 runs a route

## 5. Three-Play Scripting

### Opening Drive (Start at own 5-yard line, Closed)

The first 3 plays are scripted. No audibles. No debates. Execute.

**Option A: Storm-Thunder-Fades**

| Play # | Status | Call | Target | Expected Result |

## 7. Defensive Strategy

### Base Defense: 2-1-2 Zone

```
         [End Zone / Offense this way -->]

     D1 (F)         D3 (M)          D2 (F)       <-- Deep zone (10+ yds)
                                                      D1/D2 are your females
                     D4 (M)                       <-- Middle zone (5-10 yds)

                     D5 (M)                       <-- Short / Rush zone (LOS to 5 yds)
                                                      Also the designated rusher
         ============ LOS ============
```

- **D1 and D2 (Females):** Cover the deep halves. On Closed plays, one of them must key on the offense's primary female receiver.
- **D3 (Male):** Deep middle safety. Reads the QB eyes. Covers the seam.
- **D4 (Male):** Middle zone linebacker. Covers drags, curls, and crossing routes at 5-10 yards.
- **D5 (Male or Female):** Short zone. If a female: can rush from beyond 7-yard mark. If male: plays the LOS area.

### Adjustments by Offensive Play Status

**When Offense Is CLOSED:**
- They MUST involve a female. Key on their females.
- D1 and D2 tighten coverage on the offensive females.
- D4 watches for female drags/crosses underneath.
- D5 watches for hand-offs to a female.
- Cheat toward wherever their best female lines up.

**When Offense Is OPEN:**
- They have full freedom. Play honest zone.
- Respect the deep routes (M1/M2 can get the ball freely).
- D3 stays deep. Do not bite on underneath routes.
- D5 reads for the run -- if they hand off, all defenders can rush (no 7-yard rule after hand-off).

### Anti-Double-Pass Read

The double pass (hand-off to F1, F1 throws) is the most dangerous play in coed flag football. To defend it:

1. **D5 (spy):** Watch the player who receives the hand-off. Do NOT rush past them. Stay square.
2. **Read the shoulders:** If the hand-off receiver turns their shoulders downfield to run, play the run. If their shoulders stay open (facing downfield receivers), they are about to throw.
3. **D4:** When you see a hand-off, do NOT crash toward the ball carrier. Stay in your zone and look for receivers coming into your area.
4. **D1/D2:** On a hand-off, maintain depth. The throw is coming.

### Rush Strategy

- Only females can rush from beyond the 7-yard marker.
- After a hand-off, everyone can rush (7-yard rule no longer in effect).
- Use **F1 as the primary rusher** on defense when she is not needed in coverage.
- **Fake rush:** Have F2 line up at the rush mark, take one step forward at snap, then drop into coverage. Creates hesitation in the QB.

## 8. Clock and Game Management

### The One Timeout

You get ONE timeout (60 seconds, clock stops). Use it wisely.

| Use it when... | Do NOT use it when... |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Trailing late, need to stop clock | Leading -- let the clock run |
| Team is confused about a play call | Just because you're tired |
| Need to adjust after seeing a defensive look | Early in the game (save it) |
| Before a critical PAT decision | After a bad play to "regroup" -- just huddle faster |
| Opponent scores to tie/take lead with < 2 min left | On defense -- you can't stop their clock |

### Pace Management

**When LEADING:**
- Use the full 25-second play clock every snap
- Huddle slowly, walk to the line
- Run safe, short plays (Out, Levels, Screen Queen) -- eat clock and avoid turnovers
- A 3-play possession that takes 75 seconds of clock without scoring is a WIN

**When TRAILING:**
- Sprint to the line after every play
- Call plays in the huddle while walking to the line, not at the line
- Take deep shots (Storm, Thunder, Fades) -- you need points, not clock
- Use the timeout to stop the clock on YOUR possession, not theirs

### First Possession Scripting

Winning the coin toss: **Take the ball first.**
- You get a scripted 3-play opening drive (Storm-Thunder-Fades or Out-Fades-Lightning)
- You set the tempo and force the opponent to react
- Scoring first in single elimination puts enormous pressure on the other team

If you lose the toss and they take the ball:
- Play base 2-1-2 defense
- Do not gamble on defense in the first possession
- Get a stop, then run your scripted opening drive

## 9. Trick Play Legality Quick Reference

| Play / Technique | Legal? | Notes |
|------------------|--------|-------|
| Double pass (hand-off behind LOS, then throw) | **LEGAL** | Throw must come from behind LOS |
| Fake rush then receive/throw | **LEGAL** | Player must be behind LOS to throw |
| QB becomes receiver after hand-off | **LEGAL** | QB is eligible once they hand off the ball |
| Stacked/bunched formation | **LEGAL** | No blocking, but legal positioning |
| NRZ hand-off behind LOS then pass | **LEGAL** | Hand-off is behind LOS; pass goes forward beyond LOS |
| Multiple hand-offs behind LOS | **LEGAL** | No limit on hand-offs behind LOS |
| QB runs the ball | **ILLEGAL** | Even if QB is female |
| Motion toward LOS at snap | **ILLEGAL** | Motion must be parallel or away from LOS at snap moment |
| Lateral / pitch | **ILLEGAL** | Only direct hand-offs behind LOS; no laterals of any kind |
| Forward pass received behind LOS | **ILLEGAL** | All passes must be received beyond LOS |
| Male advancing ball across LOS by running | **ILLEGAL** | Only females may rush across LOS |
| More than 1 player in motion | **ILLEGAL** | Only 1 player may be in motion pre-snap |
| Blocking / pick plays with contact | **ILLEGAL** | No contact of any kind; positioning without contact is fine |
| Jumping by ball carrier | **ILLEGAL** | Ball spotted at point of jump |
| Diving | **ILLEGAL** | Cannot leave feet to avoid a defender |
| Interception return | **NOT ALLOWED** | Ball is dead at point of interception |
| Flag guarding (arm over flags while running) | **ILLEGAL** | Offensive penalty, 10 yards + loss of down |
